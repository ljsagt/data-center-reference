Important But No Score Change

  • HBM / DRAM: LTA terms, price revisions, and process complexity all reinforced bottleneck and pricing scores already at 5.
  • MLCC / ABF: AI mix and pricing recovery improved, but moat evidence did not cross a new threshold.
  • CoWoS: TSMC-led CoWoS-L remained a gating capability; China-local under-utilization was treated separately.

  • 800V power: The 800V architecture theme grew stronger, but current evidence still maps broadly to power-semiconductor and watchlist items.

Watchlist

  • Optical fiber, cable, CPO and NPO: Demand and pricing evidence strengthened outside the current laser/transceiver rows.
  • 800V rack power: SST, SSCB, busbar, and power-shelf components need cleaner row mapping.
  • Rack-level BBU: Panasonic-style distributed BBU architecture may deserve a dedicated cell/module row.
  • Custom ASIC/IP lock-in: SerDes and die-to-die IP ownership is economically powerful but not a current component row.
  • PCIe/CXL chips: Retimers, CXL timing, and Broadcom SUE remain visible but unmapped.
  • Regional packaging split: China-local OSAT evidence should not be averaged with TSMC/ex-China CoWoS tightness.
