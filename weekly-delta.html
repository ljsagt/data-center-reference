Pricing Power

  • pricingHDI multilayer PCB: 3 → 4. GB300 suppliers are passing through roughly 10% material inflation after a one-to-two-quarter lag. Another expert described a USD2-2.5 increase on a USD5 board, with material costs still absorbing part of the gain.
  • pricingMLCC stayed at 5. The latest checks put the high-capacitance gap near 20% and described another 20-30% price increase opportunity, but pricing was already at the maximum score.
  • pricingCoWoS and broad optics stayed at 4. Capacity and demand remain strong, but the evidence did not show a new component-specific price move that clears the scoring rule.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckNo bottleneck score changed. The new evidence confirmed tight supply, but affected components were already at 5 or the constraint is not yet in volume production.
  • bottleneckHDI PCB stayed at 5. Nvidia-qualified capacity remains narrow in 2026, though broader industry additions from H1 2027 could start to ease allocation.
  • bottleneckGlass core stayed at 3. TGV and plating tools take more than a year, but mass production is still expected only in late 2027 or 2028.

Moat

  • moatGlass core package substrate: 3 → 4. Thick glass needs fine TGV drilling, void-free plating, 2x2-5x5μm patterning, 100% inspection, and months of assembly and reliability testing.
  • moatHDI PCB stayed at 4. Qualification and plant-specific yield know-how remain important, but top-ten capacity growth and likely 2027 price competition stop a move to 5.
  • moatPower semiconductors stayed at 4. Qualified SiC has a real reliability barrier, but the broad row still includes more competitive IGBT, MOSFET, and GaN paths.

Important But No Score Change

  • MLCC: High-capacitance supply is about 20% short, and Japanese equipment takes 12-16 months, but the row was already 5 / 5 / 5.
  • HBM / CoWoS: Supplier ASPs, capacity reservations, and higher TSMC capex reinforced existing ceiling or near-ceiling scores.
  • Low-loss laminate: GB300 material inflation is being passed through, but this row was already 5 / 5 / 4.
  • Optics / AEC: Lumentum capacity is full beyond 2028, while second-source DSPs could halve AEC prices; the mixed evidence did not justify a broad score change.
  • 800V power: SiC earns a reliability premium, but Rohm showed no broad price increase and multiple device paths keep the broad moat below 5.

Watchlist

  • MLCC production inputs: Sub-120nm nickel/ceramic powder, rare-earth oxides, tape-casting tools, and lamination equipment.
  • AEC / NPO / interconnect chips: Credo AEC, second-source DSPs, PCIe/CXL retimers, and custom SerDes IP.
  • Thick-glass TGV equipment: Glass-specific drilling, plating, fine patterning, and 100% inspection.
  • SiC substrate and module packaging: Boule yield, 200mm conversion, double-sided cooling, and high-voltage qualification.
  • Grid and bridge power: Utility, gas interconnect, substations, and switchgear still take 12-24 months or more.
  • Custom-ASIC reserved capacity: Broadcom, HBM, foundry, packaging, SerDes, ODM, and optics reservations create a bundled bottleneck.
