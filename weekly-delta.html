Weekly Incremental Change

2026-05-29 refresh versus 2026-05-23 baseline

One Page Summary

  • No scores went down this week.
  • The biggest upgrade was ABF package substrate. It moved up in bottleneck, pricing power, and moat.
  • Pricing power improved most in ABF, HBM, and MLCC because this week's sources showed stronger ASP, mix, or price contract evidence.
  • Bottleneck risk increased in ABF substrates, MLCC, power semiconductors, CPO lasers/modules, and dry-type transformers.
  • Moat improved only where the evidence showed hard qualification barriers or difficult manufacturing: ABF package substrate and CPO module.

Pricing Power

  • pricingAjinomoto ABF film: Pricing moved from 4 to 5. ABF substrate ASP evidence is now explicit, and film allocation still favors major AI customers.
  • pricingABF flip-chip package substrate: Pricing moved from 4 to 5. Sources pointed to 2026 ASP up 30-40% and customer acceptance of higher prices.
  • pricingHBM: Pricing moved from 4 to 5. AI demand is pulling memory supply away from other markets, keeping memory pricing high.
  • pricingMLCC: Pricing moved from 4 to 5. Existing products are not broadly being repriced, but high-end AI products have mix-driven ASP uplift.
  • pricingDry-type transformers: Pricing moved from 3 to 4. Long lead times support stronger supplier pricing, but not enough for a moat upgrade.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckABF flip-chip package substrate: Bottleneck moved from 4 to 5. Supply gap is widening, new capacity is reserved, and key materials remain tight.
  • bottleneckMLCC: Bottleneck moved from 4 to 5. High-spec AI demand, high utilization, and conservative capacity additions point to tighter supply.
  • bottleneckPower semiconductors: Bottleneck moved from 4 to 5. 800V AI power architecture and GaN/SiC shortage evidence increased pressure.
  • bottleneckExternal laser source: Bottleneck moved from 4 to 5. Hyperscaler demand is larger than legacy optical manufacturing lines were built to handle.
  • bottleneckCPO module: Bottleneck moved from 3 to 4. CPO is still early, but manufacturing capacity and optical coupling are already constraints.
  • bottleneckDry-type transformers: Bottleneck moved from 3 to 4. AIDC sources cited 72-80 week transformer lead times through 2028.

Moat

  • moatABF flip-chip package substrate: Moat moved from 3 to 4. New ABF lines take 1.5-2 years, key T-glass supply is concentrated, and high-layer yields remain difficult.
  • moatCPO module: Moat moved from 3 to 4. The evidence points to hard packaging, optical coupling, and manufacturing yield barriers.
  • moatAll other changed rows: Moat did not move. The evidence showed tighter supply or higher pricing, but not enough new qualification or process barriers.

Important But No Score Change

  • CoWoS: Still tight, but already at max bottleneck and moat scores.
  • EML, DFB, and pluggable optics: Demand was confirmed, but there was not enough new pricing evidence to move scores.
  • TLVR inductors: Demand and cost pass-through were confirmed, but there was no new allocation or lead-time data.
  • Bearings and fan motors: Demand is stronger, but new capacity is also being added.
  • OLTC and EHV transformer stack: System-level transformer shortage was confirmed, but OLTC-specific scores were already high.

Watchlist

  • Liquid cold plates / MLCP: Important evidence, but the current scorecard has no dedicated cold-plate row.
  • 800V HVDC / solid-state transformer: Important architecture change, but only partly captured by current power semiconductor and transformer rows.
  • Behind-the-meter power: Important system constraint, but too broad to assign to one engine, switchgear, or fuel-system component.
