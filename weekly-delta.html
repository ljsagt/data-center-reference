Pricing Power

  • pricingABF / substrates: Pricing stayed at 5. New Ibiden and AT&S commentary pushed ASP expectations higher again, but both ABF rows were already maxed.
  • pricingHBM: Pricing stayed at 5. HBM4E samples, export growth, and price-catch-up commentary all reinforced the thesis.
  • pricingMLCC: Pricing stayed at 5. New checks pointed to 20-40% price pressure and AI-server lead times near 24 weeks.
  • pricingPower semiconductors: Pricing stayed at 5. VRM and SPS pricing still looks up 10-20%+ even if HVDC timing shifts modestly.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckCoWoS: Bottleneck stayed at 5. TSMC still looks like the main near-term packaging choke point.
  • bottleneckHBM: Bottleneck stayed at 5. Sample shipments improved visibility, but supply is still racing demand rather than catching up.
  • bottleneckLPDDR5X / SOCAMM: Bottleneck stayed at 5. New checks still show Vera-class demand pulling directly on the mobile-memory supply base.
  • bottleneckMLCC: Bottleneck stayed at 5. AI-server content and lead times remain consistent with a structurally tight passive stack.

Moat

  • moatABF substrate: Moat stayed at 5. The latest ABF and EMIB-T notes still point to hard qualification and execution barriers around the current leaders.
  • moatCoWoS: Moat stayed at 5. EMIB-T broadened the advanced-packaging story, but it does not reduce TSMC's near-term strategic position.
  • moatHBM: Moat stayed at 4. Supplier concentration remains strong, but the row still covers multiple qualified memory makers.
  • moatPower semiconductors: Moat stayed at 4. SiC remains concentrated, but the broad row still includes wider GaN, MOSFET, and IGBT competition.

Important But No Score Change

  • ABF and AI package substrates: ASP and demand commentary strengthened again, but both ABF rows were already at 15.
  • CoWoS: Still the main near-term packaging bottleneck, but the row already had max bottleneck and moat scores.
  • HBM: Sample shipments and stronger pricing commentary reinforced scarcity, but HBM was already maxed on bottleneck and pricing.
  • MLCC: Lead-time and pricing evidence improved, but bottleneck and pricing were already 5.
  • Power semiconductors: Demand and pricing improved while HVDC timing slipped somewhat, but the broad row was already maxed on bottleneck and pricing.
  • LPDDR5X / SOCAMM: Demand stayed strong, but Bernstein's view that SOCAMM2 remains niche kept the moat score unchanged.

Watchlist

  • EMIB-T and other non-CoWoS package paths: Important enough to monitor, but not represented as a separate row.
  • PCIe retimers, PCIe switches, RCD and MRCD chips: Still relevant, and new SOCAMM2 notes did not justify removing them from watchlist.
  • BMC chips: Aspeed and server-control-chip evidence still maps to architecture, but no BMC row exists today.
  • 800V HVDC equipment: SST, SSCB, BBU/CBU, and power-shelf components still need dedicated rows if they become core constraints.
  • Advanced-packaging wet tools and AI test cells: Still important, but not yet directly represented in the table.
