Weekly Incremental Change

2026-06-15 refresh versus 2026-06-12 baseline

One Page Summary

  • This refresh used 2 local sell-side summary files and the June 15 Third Bridge package with 46 transcript exports.
  • Four existing scorecard rows changed: low-loss PCB laminate, high-frequency copper foil, LPDDR5X/SOCAMM memory, and ABF flip-chip package substrate.
  • The biggest change was CCL and copper foil. New evidence showed real lead-time pressure, price hikes, and harder qualification barriers.
  • Memory also tightened. LPDDR5X supply is only about 60% of demand, and prices could rise sharply in 2Q.
  • HBM, MLCC, CoWoS, and power semiconductors were refreshed with strong evidence, but those rows were already near their ceilings.

Pricing Power

  • pricingLow-loss PCB laminate: Pricing rose from 4 to 5. E-glass prices doubled, Kingboard is raising prices often, EMC raised M6-and-below products by 40-60%, and M7/M8 prices rose 10-20%.
  • pricingHigh-frequency copper foil: Pricing rose from 3 to 4. Processing fees and copper prices are expected to keep rising in H2 as suppliers shift toward high-end HVLP3/4 products.
  • pricingLPDDR5X / SOCAMM: Pricing rose from 4 to 5. Citi/TrendForce said LPDDR5X prices could rise as much as 80% in 2Q because AI server and AI PC demand is overwhelming supply.
  • pricingMLCC and HBM: Pricing stayed at 5. New evidence was positive, but both rows were already at the ceiling.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckLow-loss PCB laminate: Bottleneck rose from 4 to 5. CCL raw materials are tight across copper foil, resin, and glass cloth; M6-and-below lead times are 8-16 weeks.
  • bottleneckHigh-frequency copper foil: Bottleneck rose from 3 to 4. Taiwan Copper Foil and Co-tech are moving capacity away from lower grades, while Nvidia is chasing Co-tech HVLP4 volume.
  • bottleneckLPDDR5X / SOCAMM: Bottleneck rose from 4 to 5. Current supply is only about 60% of demand as Vera Rubin and AI PC demand ramps.
  • bottleneckHBM: Bottleneck stayed at 5. Third Bridge said HBM4 is the tightest memory point and shortages could cascade through 2028.

Moat

  • moatABF substrate: Moat rose from 4 to 5. The new evidence showed ABF core-material quality, T-glass supply, and warpage control are difficult for new entrants to match.
  • moatLow-loss PCB laminate: Moat rose from 3 to 4. Nan Ya's integrated glass cloth, copper foil, and CCL position matters because customers can avoid requalifying many separate suppliers.
  • moatHigh-frequency copper foil: Moat rose from 3 to 4. The pressure is in high-grade HVLP3/4 capacity, not generic foil.
  • moatPower semiconductors: Moat stayed at 4. SiC looks concentrated, but the broad row still includes GaN, MOSFET, and IGBT supply where competition is wider.

Important But No Score Change

  • HBM: Still very tight, but already maxed at 5 for bottleneck and pricing.
  • CoWoS: TSMC still had to redirect some SoIC capacity back to CoWoS for Nvidia urgent orders, but the CoWoS row was already at 5 bottleneck and 5 moat.
  • MLCC: Sell-side notes were very bullish on AI-server MLCC demand, but bottleneck and pricing were already 5.
  • Power semiconductors: 800V HVDC and SiC evidence stayed strong, but the row was already 5 bottleneck and 5 pricing.

Watchlist

  • E-glass / T-glass / Q-glass cloth: Evidence is strong enough to monitor as a possible separate row.
  • PCIe retimers, PCIe switches, RCD and MRCD chips: Good evidence, but the scorecard does not yet have exact rows.
  • BMC chips: Aspeed/Nuvoton evidence maps to AI server architecture, but no BMC row exists today.
  • 800V HVDC equipment: SST, SSCB, BBU/CBU, and power-shelf components may need their own rows.
  • Advanced-packaging wet tools and AI test cells: Grand Process, Scientech, Cohu, Teradyne, and silicon-photonics test evidence is important but not yet directly scored.
