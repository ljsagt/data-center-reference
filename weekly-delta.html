Pricing Power

  • pricingCoWoS: 4 → 5. Amkor's CoWoS-R capacity is fully booked through 2027, and supply tightness is now supporting price increases in both packaging and test.
  • pricingHBM stayed at 5. HBM demand and supplier ASPs remain strong. Separate server-DRAM and NAND price signals reinforce memory tightness but cannot be mapped to the HBM row.
  • pricingABF substrates stayed at 5. Unimicron says scarce, larger, more complex substrates are raising ASP per area faster than ordinary price increases.
  • pricing800G optical transceivers stayed at 4. Pricing is holding and EML share is rising, but the note does not show an explicit transceiver price increase.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckNo bottleneck score changed. The new evidence confirmed tight supply, but each directly affected component was already at 5.
  • bottleneckEML lasers stayed at 5. TFC Optical reported that EML material constraints continued during the second quarter; vertical integration helped it protect output.
  • bottleneckABF substrates stayed at 5. Nittobo remains the effective sole source for high-end T-glass, while AT&S expects strong ABF shipments and is considering more Kulim expansion.
  • bottleneckCoWoS stayed at 5. Amkor is fully booked through 2027 while board supply still limits MediaTek's 2026 TPU upside; better availability is a later relief signal, not evidence that today's bottleneck has cleared.

Moat

  • moatHigh-frequency copper foil: 4 → 5. Mitsui retains about 80% global share in HVLP4+ foil, and July competitor results did not show meaningful erosion in its highest-end position.
  • moatABF substrates stayed at 5. Nittobo's effective sole-source T-glass position and substrate reliability requirements reconfirm the existing ceiling score.
  • moatPower semiconductors stayed at 4. SiC substrate prices are firming again, but the broad row still mixes several competitive device technologies and suppliers.

Important But No Score Change

  • MLCC: AI accelerator demand pushed the book-to-bill ratio to its highest level since the pandemic, but the row was already 5 / 5 / 5.
  • HBM: Strong HBM demand and new server-memory price signals reinforced the existing 5 / 5 / 4 score.
  • EML lasers: 800G pricing is holding and EML share is rising, but bottleneck, pricing, and moat were already at 5.
  • ABF substrates: Unimicron's scarce large, multilayer substrate mix confirmed the existing 5 / 5 / 5 score.
  • Low-loss PCB materials: Panasonic continues to cite Rubin and custom-ASIC rack board materials as a growth driver, but gave no new lead-time, allocation, or pricing trigger.
  • 800V power: SiC substrate pricing is improving, but the broad component row still includes competing IGBT, MOSFET, and GaN paths.

Watchlist

  • Server DRAM: A 64GB module spot quote moved above USD3,000 versus a USD1,300 second-quarter contract benchmark, and GM now cites DRAM as a second-half cost headwind; the table has no standalone server-DRAM row.
  • Enterprise SSD and NAND: Citi expects third-quarter NAND pricing above 30% sequentially and enterprise SSD pricing around 50% higher, while suppliers shift capacity toward HBM and DRAM.
  • Nearline HDD heads and media: Data-center storage checks point to higher Seagate and Western Digital sourcing from TDK and Resonac, but these inputs lack dedicated rows.
  • AI rack rail kits: King Slide's second-quarter revenue rose 156% year over year on GB300, TPU, and Trainium project pull-ins.
  • Liquid-cooling test equipment: Dedicated CDU test systems carry about a USD200,000 ASP, but the table does not isolate test equipment from cooling hardware.
  • SiC substrate and module packaging: Six-inch substrate pricing is firming as AI and EV 800V demand improves, while qualification and packaging remain separate constraints.
  • AI rack BBU systems: Panasonic cited new BBU awards alongside Rubin and ASIC rack demand, but did not identify the constrained scored subcomponent.
  • Silicon-photonics test equipment: Advantest reported a volume purchase order and is expanding SoC tester capacity; the table covers optical components rather than the test stack.
