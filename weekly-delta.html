2026-06-20 refresh versus 2026-06-19 baseline

One Page Summary

  • This refresh used the local June 20 Third Bridge package: one assembled topic/key-points HTML and 66 validated transcript exports. The two June 19 sell-side files were rechecked as already reflected in the baseline.
  • Three existing scorecard rows changed: EML, HDI multilayer PCB, and backplane PCB.
  • Pricing power improved only for EML. The optical expert said scarce EML can hold higher prices or secure price guarantees because customers have few alternatives.
  • Bottleneck and moat improved for AI-server PCB. Rubin-class HDI and 56/78-layer backplanes are harder to build, qualified suppliers are limited, and new HDI capacity takes at least three quarters.
  • ABF, CoWoS, HBM, MLCC, and power semiconductors stayed very strong, but their scorecard rows were already at ceiling or near-ceiling levels.
  • Watch the late-2027 risk: optical modules and PCB both have overbooking or substitution risk if supply catches demand or CPO/glass/ceramic paths reduce PCB content.

Pricing Power

  • pricingEML: Pricing moved from 4 to 5. Supply is short, customers have few alternatives, and the expert said EML can sell at higher prices or receive price guarantees.
  • pricingMLCC: Stayed at 5. The expert said embedded MLCC value is small versus the compute board, so even large price moves can be absorbed by Google or Nvidia.
  • pricingAI-server PCB: Stayed at 3. Raw materials are rising, but Nvidia is pushing PCB suppliers to absorb CCL, copper, and E-glass costs.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckHDI multilayer PCB: Bottleneck moved from 4 to 5. Nvidia-grade HDI/mSAP capacity is narrow, and new HDI capacity takes at least three quarters to add.
  • bottleneckBackplane PCB: Bottleneck moved from 3 to 4. Rubin 56/78-layer orthogonal backplanes face warpage, signal-integrity, and yield issues.
  • bottleneckCoWoS / HBM / ABF: Stayed tight. Google TPU and custom ASIC checks again showed these as gating resources, but the scores were already high.

Moat

  • moatHDI multilayer PCB: Moat moved from 3 to 4. Fewer than ten listed Chinese PCB makers were described as having both HDI and mSAP process capability.
  • moatBackplane PCB: Moat moved from 3 to 4. Victory Giant is expected to keep about 70% share in core Rubin orthogonal backplanes because stability and qualification matter.
  • moatSilicon photonics: No score change. 1.6T silicon-photonics yield is difficult, but the evidence was module-level rather than a clean MZM component shortage.

Important But No Score Change

  • ABF: Google TPU COT checks still point to multi-year ABF scarcity, but ABF was already 15.
  • CoWoS: Quota remains a gating resource, but bottleneck and moat were already maxed.
  • HBM: HBM4E cost inflation supports pricing, but pricing and bottleneck were already 5.
  • MLCC: 5x capacitance density migration and price absorption strengthen the thesis, but bottleneck and pricing were already 5.
  • Power semiconductors / 800V: Demand evidence improved, but the broad row stays below max moat because competition remains wider outside SiC/GaN niches.

Watchlist

  • SiTime / timing chips: Higher timing content and sticky clock-tree design wins, but no dedicated timing-chip row exists.
  • Custom ASIC and CPU alternatives: Qualcomm Oryon, Google TPU, AWS silicon, and MediaTek matter for architecture, but not as component bottleneck rows.
  • PCB substitution: Thin-film ceramic circuits, glass substrates, ceramic substrates, all-optical networks, and CPO could reduce PCB intensity after the Rubin wave.
  • 800V HVDC equipment: SST, SSCB, BBU/CBU, and power-shelf architecture still need dedicated rows if evidence becomes component-level.
