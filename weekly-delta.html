Pricing Power

  • pricingHigh-frequency copper foil: 4 → 5. 1.6T optical modules use carrier copper in 14 of 16 layers. Supply is expected to stay short through late 2027, while newer HVLP generations rise from roughly RMB 200k per tonne toward RMB 250k and potentially RMB 350k.
  • pricingMLCC stayed at 5. High-capacitance spot prices and AI mix rose sharply, but pricing was already at the maximum score.
  • pricingBroad pluggable transceivers stayed at 4. 800G and 1.6T demand exceeds supply, but same-generation module prices still fall and multi-sourcing limits broad supplier pricing power.

Bottleneck

  • bottleneckHigh-frequency copper foil: 4 → 5. Higher-speed optics add much more premium carrier copper, and the latest checks show a shortage lasting through Q4 2027.
  • bottleneckCPO module: 4 → 5. Systems are already shipping, but first-generation optical test tools process only about two wafers per day. The market needs hundreds of tools by Q1 2027, up from dozens in H1 2026.
  • bottleneckABF and CoWoS stayed at 5. New evidence showed ABF demand near three times prior demand, low initial yields, and a roughly 20% CoWoS supply gap, but both rows were already at the ceiling.

Moat

  • moatMultilayer ceramic capacitor: 4 → 5. Nvidia-grade parts require sub-1μm sheets, more than 1,200 stacked layers, ultra-pure powder, specialized Japanese equipment, and difficult yields. Three suppliers control about 90% of high-capacitance supply.
  • moatCPO module: 4 → 5. Fiber placement needs at least 0.1μm accuracy—about 100 times tighter than conventional CMOS handling—plus dual-sided testing and new OSAT production skills.
  • moatPower semiconductors stayed at 4. 800V raises SiC/GaN content, but falling SiC wafer prices and several competing device paths prevent a broad maximum moat score.

Important But No Score Change

  • HBM / DRAM: Contract pricing and allocation stayed strong, but bottleneck and pricing were already at 5.
  • ABF / CoWoS: Yield loss, limited advanced capacity, and larger packages reinforced the current ceiling bottleneck scores.
  • EML / InP: Laser supply remains tight for one to two years, but the EML row was already 5 / 5 / 5.
  • 800V power: Content per rack rises sharply, while safety standards, dual-source qualification, and new sidecar/SST designs delay the ramp.
  • Liquid cooling: Cold plates, CDUs, sensors, pumps, and valves are growing quickly, but evidence is spread across sockets and does not map cleanly to one row.

Watchlist

  • CPO production equipment: Dual-sided testers, 0.1μm alignment stages, die handling, and OSAT throughput.
  • Liquid-cooling hardware: Cold plates, manifolds, quick connectors, and qualified rack-level sensors, pumps, and valves.
  • Grid and large power equipment: Utility connections above three years, transformers above 10MVA beyond one year, and MV switchgear prices up 7-8% QoQ.
  • 800V rack chain: Sidecars, solid-state transformers, safety standards, busbars, and rack power shelves.
  • AEC / NPO / interconnect chips: Credo AEC pricing, NPO serviceability, PCIe/CXL retimers, and custom SerDes IP.
  • CoWoP / TGV migration: Value may move from ABF into mSAP PCB, TGV drilling/fill, and OSAT lines from 2027 onward.
